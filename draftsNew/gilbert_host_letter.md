Dear Members of the Evaluation Committee,

I am writing in strong support of the application submitted by **Dr Sigríður Sunna Ebenesersdóttir** (Adjunct Professor, University of Iceland) for a Marie Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship (MSCA-PF) to be hosted in my research group at the Centre for Evolutionary Hologenomics (CEH), GLOBE Institute, University of Copenhagen.

**My background and the host environment**

I am a Professor at the Centre for Evolutionary Hologenomics, GLOBE Institute, University of Copenhagen. My research group works at the intersection of ancient and environmental genomics, with a particular focus on the population genomics of animals. CEH is one of the world's leading centres for ancient DNA research, with state-of-the-art ancient DNA laboratory facilities, high-performance computing infrastructure, and a highly international and multidisciplinary research environment. The centre has a strong track record of hosting and training postdoctoral researchers from across the world.

**Why I wish to host Dr Ebenesersdóttir**

Dr Ebenesersdóttir is an outstanding researcher with a rare combination of technical and scientific expertise. Her track record speaks for itself: she led the sequencing and analysis of 88 ancient Icelandic human genomes published in *Science* (2018), and her ongoing work on 201 ancient Icelandic human genomes represents one of the most comprehensive ancient population genomic studies of any isolated human population. She brings to CEH something our group currently lacks: deep, hands-on expertise in ancient DNA extraction and sequencing from Icelandic skeletal material, combined with exclusive access to archaeological horse specimens from Iceland's National Museum — a resource that is simply unavailable to any other research group.

**The proposed project**

The project Dr Ebenesersdóttir proposes — generating the first genome-wide ancient DNA dataset for the Icelandic horse, spanning 1,100 years from the Viking Age founding to the late 19th century — is scientifically outstanding and highly timely. The Icelandic horse is one of the most remarkable model systems in domestic animal biology: more than a millennium of legally enforced isolation, a near-complete historical record, and a distinctive suite of adaptations make it uniquely suited to answering fundamental questions about the genomic consequences of founder effects, drift, and selective breeding. No other group is currently positioned to carry out this project; the combination of Dr Ebenesersdóttir's aDNA expertise and our group's computational and analytical infrastructure makes CEH the natural home for it.

All aDNA wet laboratory work will be conducted at CEH using specimens transported from the National Museum of Iceland under permission from the Icelandic cultural heritage authority. Dr Ebenesersdóttir will carry out extraction, library preparation, and initial quality assessment in our ancient DNA clean-room facility using protocols she developed and optimised for degraded Icelandic skeletal material. Sequencing and all downstream bioinformatic and population genomic analyses will be performed at CEH. The project also benefits from an established scientific collaboration with Prof. Ludovic Orlando (CAGT, University of Toulouse), whose laboratory has developed the largest ancient horse imputation reference panel in the world (>900 genomes) and will contribute horse genomics expertise and comparative data.

**What CEH will provide**

Dr Ebenesersdóttir will have full access to:

- CEH ancient DNA laboratory facilities and reagents for DNA extraction and library preparation
- High-performance computing infrastructure for large-scale population genomic analyses
- The expertise of our group in population genomics of animals, directly applicable to the analytical objectives of this project
- Regular one-to-one supervision and participation in group meetings, journal clubs, and CEH seminars
- Support for training in population genomic methods, including genome-wide selection scans and demographic modelling
- CEH's extensive international network and conference participation opportunities

I will personally supervise Dr Ebenesersdóttir throughout the fellowship and ensure she has the scientific environment, resources, and mentoring needed to complete this project and to develop her independent research profile.

**Career development**

The MSCA fellowship represents an important step in Dr Ebenesersdóttir's career trajectory. Moving from Iceland to Copenhagen will broaden her international network, expose her to a new scientific community with complementary expertise, and position her to establish an independent research programme in ancient animal genomics — a field in which she is already making foundational contributions. I am fully committed to supporting her career development through this fellowship and beyond.

**Conclusion**

Dr Ebenesersdóttir is an exceptional candidate for an MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowship. The project she proposes is scientifically compelling, feasible, and timely, and CEH is the ideal environment in which to carry it out. I give this application my unreserved support and look forward to welcoming her to Copenhagen.
