## Section A — Specific Aims

### "Celtic pony hypothesis" — Aim 1

> "a distribution that has long suggested Celtic pony contributed to Iceland's founding horse population, the so-called 'Celtic pony hypothesis'"

The term "Celtic pony hypothesis" is used in the literature (Aðalsteinsson & Sigurðsson, 1998), and keeping it in quotation marks as a named historical concept is defensible. However, the phrase "Celtic pony contributed to Iceland's founding horse population" should be reworded. The modern genomic evidence (DMRT3 in Jorvik horses, phylogenetic affinity with Pictish specimens, BRIT ancestry component in British native breeds) points to horses from Britain specifically, not to "Celtic" populations from Ireland and Wales in particular. A reviewer who knows the field may push back on the "Celtic pony" framing now that better genomic evidence is available.

Suggested revision:

> "a distribution that has long suggested a British Isles contribution to the founding horse population, sometimes called the 'Celtic pony hypothesis' (Aðalsteinsson & Sigurðsson, 1998)"

### Citation placement — Aim 1

> "Phylogenetic analysis further groups the Icelandic horse with the Shetland pony in a shared pony clade (Petersen et al., 2013), and genomic analysis identifies a common Iron Age ancestry component shared between Icelandic horses and Dartmoor and Exmoor ponies — together pointing to a British Isles contribution to the founding stock Ahmed et al. The citation at the end of this sentence is missing its parentheses. It reads as if "Ahmed et al. (under review)" is continuing the sentence rather than being a citation. Fix to:

### "Wutke et al. also examined ancient Icelandic horses, but for a single coat colour locus only" — Originality section

> "Wutke et al. (2016) also examined ancient Icelandic horses, but for a single coat colour locus only."

This is an error. Wutke et al. (2016) examined the **DMRT3 gait variant** (the ambling/tölt locus), not a coat colour locus. Coat colour (PMEL17/Silver) is a different study altogether. The gait locus is discussed extensively and correctly earlier in the document; this sentence in the Originality section contradicts that. Fix to:

> "Wutke et al. (2016) also examined ancient Icelandic horses, but for a single gait locus only (DMRT3)."

The following citations appear in the text but are **not in the reference list**:

2. **Norton et al. (2019)** — cited in Aim 3 for HMGA2 as a replicated EMS locus across native pony breeds. Not in bibliography. The likely reference is: Norton EM, Mickelson JR, McCue ME, Rendahl AK (2019). *Genome-wide association study of equine metabolic syndrome in Welsh ponies and Morgan horses.* Animal Genetics, 50(1), 14–23. https://doi.org/10.1111/age.12739 — verify before adding.

3. **Rohland & Hofreiter (2007)** — cited in Section C for silica-based DNA extraction. Not in bibliography. The likely reference is: Rohland N, Hofreiter M (2007). *Comparison and optimization of ancient DNA extraction.* BioTechniques, 42(3), 343–352.
