### Strontium isotope analysis — University of Durham

87Sr/86Sr isotope analysis of second and third molars from selected ancient horse specimens. The University of Durham is the same laboratory currently performing strontium analysis for the in-preparation ancient Icelandic human genomics study (Ebenesersdóttir et al., in preparation); the Icelandic geological baseline is already established. Approximately 60-80 specimens will be selected from Phase 1 for strontium analysis, prioritising the earliest time points (870-1100 CE) and sites shared with the human genomics study.

## Notes for finalisation

1. **SSE salary rate:** The 1,000,000 kr/month full-time employer cost is an estimate based on SSE's previous rate at deCODE Genetics (1,170,000 kr/month). Confirm the exact rate against the current collective wage agreement at the University of Iceland before submission.

2. **AH salary:** All figures in the AH section are provisional until Open Item 17 is resolved. If Agnar holds a 100% UoI position, only uncommitted time can be charged to the grant. If he holds a part-time position, a higher contribution may be possible.

3. **Free sequencing:** If deCODE provides sequencing at no cost under the collaboration agreement, or if Prof. Orlando's laboratory provides sequencing as in-kind support, reduce the sequencing lines accordingly. The freed budget should increase the contingency or, if substantial, increase SSE's salary duration.

4. **Sequencing at deCODE vs contracted services:** Since UoI is the accounting institution (not deCODE), sequencing at deCODE must be budgeted as a contracted service unless the collaboration agreement specifies otherwise. Clarify with deCODE finance before submission.

5. **Operational costs cap:** IRF guidelines state operational costs should generally not exceed 20% of the total grant (~12,260,000 kr on a 61.3M grant). Reagents (2,500,000) + sequencing (6,000,000) + strontium (3,000,000) = 11,500,000 kr = 19.2% of 60,000,000 kr. This is within the limit but close — do not add further operational costs without checking.

6. **Master's student salary:** IRF allows up to 12 months. This budget includes 6 months as stated in Section F. If a student is recruited earlier, the line can be extended to a maximum of 12 months (additional 2,700,000 kr) drawing from contingency, with IRF approval.

7. **JWW compensation:** No salary is charged for Joe Walser. His osteological work is covered by the National Museum as an in-kind contribution. If the Museum or JWW requests formal compensation, add a contracted services line of approximately 500,000-1,000,000 kr and reduce the contingency accordingly.
