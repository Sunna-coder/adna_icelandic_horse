## 2. Cited in the text but MISSING from the bibliography

These are referenced in the text but have no matching entry in the bibliography. They must be added before submission.

**Sabeti et al. (2007), Voight et al. (2006), Yi et al. (2010)** — XP-EHH, iHS, and PBS are named in Section C ("Phase 3 applies genome-wide selection scans (XP-EHH, iHS, PBS)") without inline citations. All three papers are in the bibliography, but there are no citation markers in the text. Add inline citations: XP-EHH (Sabeti et al., 2007), iHS (Voight et al., 2006), PBS (Yi et al., 2010).

**Relate / tsinfer** — Section A (Aim 2) describes building an ancestral recombination graph (ARG) and mentions it can answer questions about when DMRT3 reached fixation. The specific tools for ARG inference (Relate: Speidel et al., 2019, Nature Genetics; tsinfer: Kelleher et al., 2019, Nature Genetics) are not cited and neither paper is in the bibliography. Add citations and bibliography entries for both.

**Maier et al. (2023) — ADMIXTOOLS2** — Section C cites "admixture graph modelling (ADMIXTOOLS2; Patterson et al., 2012)." Patterson et al. (2012) is the original ADMIXTOOLS paper; ADMIXTOOLS2 is a separate tool described in Maier et al. (2023) eLife, 12, e85492. Add Maier et al. (2023) as a co-citation alongside Patterson et al. (2012), and add to the bibliography.

**TRPM1 / leopard complex** — Section A and Section F (Option 6) mention TRPM1 (leopard complex) among coat colour loci without a citation. Bellone et al. (2013) BMC Genetics is the standard reference for TRPM1 in horses. Add inline citation and bibliography entry.

**TBX3 / dun** — TBX3 (dun) is mentioned without a citation. Imsland et al. (2016) Nature Genetics is the key reference. Add inline citation and bibliography entry.

**FEIF (2025)** — Section A states "The Icelandic horse is compact — 125–145 cm at the withers" without a citation. The height range also needs updating (see §4 below). Add a citation to the FEIF breeding standards document and add the bibliography entry.

**RBST (2024)** — Section A states "Both breeds are on the critical list of the Rare Breeds Survival Trust" without a citation. Add (RBST, 2024) inline and the bibliography entry. Also see §4 regarding the accuracy of "critical list."

**Rewilding Europe** — Section A states "Exmoor ponies are now widely used in rewilding and conservation grazing projects across northern Europe" without a citation. Add a citation to the Rewilding Europe website or a relevant publication and add to the bibliography.

## 4. Text issues

**Missing spaces — two typos:**
- "likelycombined Norwegian and British pony ancestry" — should be "likely combined"
- "Noneprovides data at the depth" — should be "None provides"

**Body size — height range needs updating:**
"The Icelandic horse is compact — 125–145 cm at the withers."
125–145 cm is too wide a range and lacks a citation. Based on FEIF breeding standards, the typical range is closer to 133–143 cm. Update to: "The Icelandic horse is compact, usually standing between 133 and 143 cm at the withers (FEIF, 2025)."

**RBST — wording may not be accurate:**
"Both breeds are on the critical list of the Rare Breeds Survival Trust."
The RBST does not use a single "critical list." Their Watchlist has multiple categories. As of 2024: Exmoor = Priority, Dartmoor = At Risk. "Critical" overstates Dartmoor's status. Suggest: "both listed on the RBST Watchlist as breeds requiring conservation attention (RBST, 2024)."

**BRIT/NORD conservation argument — needs checking:**
"Ahmed et al. (under review) identified a BRIT ancestry component concentrated in critically endangered British native breeds such as the Dartmoor and Exmoor pony."

The conservation argument here implies that Icelandic horses share BRIT ancestry with Dartmoor and Exmoor and may therefore carry British-origin variation. However, Ahmed et al. characterise Icelandic horses as NORD, not BRIT. Dartmoor and Exmoor are BRIT. The genomic connection is indirect — clustering and shared ancient pony ancestry — rather than through a shared ancestry component. The sentence as written could mislead a reviewer into thinking Icelandic horses carry the BRIT component. Consider reframing around the Wutke et al. (2016) and Fages et al. (2019) evidence for a British Isles contribution to the Icelandic founding, rather than the BRIT/NORD component distinction.

**XP-EHH, iHS, PBS — need inline citations in Section C:**
"Phase 3 applies genome-wide selection scans (XP-EHH, iHS, PBS)"
No citations follow the method names. Add: XP-EHH (Sabeti et al., 2007), iHS (Voight et al., 2006), PBS (Yi et al., 2010).

**ARG — needs tool citations:**
Section A (Aim 2) describes building an ARG but does not cite the tools that would be used. Add citations for Relate (Speidel et al., 2019) and tsinfer (Kelleher et al., 2019) after "an ancestral recombination graph (ARG)."

**ADMIXTOOLS2 citation:**
"admixture graph modelling (ADMIXTOOLS2; Patterson et al., 2012)" — Patterson et al. (2012) is the original ADMIXTOOLS paper. ADMIXTOOLS2 is Maier et al. (2023). Change to "(ADMIXTOOLS2; Patterson et al., 2012; Maier et al., 2023)" and add Maier et al. (2023) to the bibliography.
