# Converting the Rannís Draft to an MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowship Application
*Notes based on comparison with THAIS (2022), ROOTS (2008), and CARIB (2013) example applications.*
*MSCA target deadline: September 2026.*

---

## Short answer: how much work is needed?

**The scientific content (roughly 60–70%) can be reused with editing. The remaining 30–40% needs to be written from scratch.** The Rannís draft is a strong scientific foundation, but the MSCA requires several whole sections that do not exist in the Rannís application at all. The biggest new writing tasks are: the training plan (what you will learn at CEH Copenhagen and why), the career development section (more detailed than Rannís Section G), the dissemination plan, and the work package structure with a Gantt chart.

## What needs to be written from scratch

### 1. Reduce scope to 24 months (MSCA is 24 months, Rannís is 36)

The three aims need to fit in 24 months, not 36. The most realistic approach:
- **Keep Aim 1** (origins/founding) fully — this is the strongest scientific and narrative hook
- **Keep Aim 2** (demographic history) — this is methodologically straightforward once the data are generated
- **Reduce Aim 3** (adaptation/traits) — either drop it entirely, or keep DMRT3 tracking only as a secondary objective within Aim 2, and remove EMS/PMEL17/IBH from the main aims. These could be framed as follow-on work for an ERC application.

The 24-month timeline also means the modern horse sequencing (100–150 horses) may be too ambitious to fit alongside the ancient DNA work. Consider whether this stays as a full aim or becomes a collaboration deliverable led by Björnsdóttir rather than the MSCA.

---

### 2. MSCA Section 1.3 — Supervision, training, and two-way knowledge transfer (DOES NOT EXIST IN RANNÍS)

This is the most important new section. It must answer:

**What will you learn at CEH Copenhagen that you cannot learn in Iceland?**
Examples from THAIS (Monnereau used this structure):
- Specific lab techniques (single-stranded library preparation? targeted enrichment?)
- Advanced bioinformatic methods (imputation with GLIMPSE2 is already in your toolkit — what does CEH add? Possibly: animal population genomics pipelines, selection scan methods used in Gilbert lab, comparative genomics across ancient animal taxa)
- Scientific independence and project management at an international institution

**What do you bring TO CEH (two-way transfer)?**
- Your expertise in ancient Icelandic populations (human + horse)
- Your co-authorship on Ahmed et al. (the horse reference panel)
- Your pipeline for working with Icelandic archaeological material
- Potentially: teaching in Tom Gilbert's courses, contributing to lab protocols

**Why Tom Gilbert / CEH specifically?**
- CEH has computing infrastructure for population genomic analyses
- Experience in ancient animal genomics (the 700,000-year horse genome came from Gilbert's lab)
- Physical separation from Iceland means genuine scientific independence (MSCA requires you to be based at the host institution)
- Gilbert has a track record of hosting MSCA fellows (the CARIB application notes that Gilbert hosted 7 Marie Curie fellows by 2013 — check current number)

This section should be approximately 600–800 words.

### 3. MSCA Section 1.4 — Researcher profile (DOES NOT EXIST AS A STANDALONE IN RANNÍS)

A short section (~300 words) summarising:
- Your PhD (University of Iceland, 2023) and what it demonstrated
- Your 27-genome and 201-genome ancient human studies
- Your co-authorship on Ahmed et al. and what it proves about your horse genomics capability
- The gap you're addressing (horse genomics, independence, international network)

This maps partly to Rannís Section G but is shorter and more focused on proving you are ready for this fellowship, not on your career goals.

---

### 4. MSCA Section 2.1 — Career development (EXISTS IN RANNÍS but needs expansion)

Rannís Section G is ~300 words. MSCA Section 2.1 needs ~400–500 words and should explicitly name:
- Specific skills to be gained (name the tools, the techniques)
- How the fellowship makes you competitive for an ERC Starting Grant
- Non-academic career options (optional but looked upon favourably — deCODE, biotech, heritage sector)
- A named Career Development Plan (CDP) to be developed with Tom Gilbert at the start of the fellowship

---

### 5. MSCA Section 2.2 — Dissemination and exploitation plan (EXISTS IN RANNÍS as Section E but needs major expansion)

Rannís Section E is ~200 words (just journal targets and data deposition). MSCA Section 2.2 requires ~700–900 words covering:

**Scientific dissemination:**
- Papers (same as Rannís — 2 articles, target journals)
- Conferences: name specific conferences with approximate dates (ISBA, SMBE, EAA)
- Data deposition (ENA, GitHub)

**Public engagement:**
- At least 2–3 named public engagement activities. Examples:
  - A public lecture in Iceland (Rannís results event, Þjóðminjasafn)
  - A blog post or media piece in collaboration with WorldFengur or FEIF
  - Participation in a Copenhagen science outreach event (e.g., Forskningens Døgn / Research Night)
  - Contribution to Tom Gilbert's existing public engagement activities

**Exploitation:**
- Who benefits (breeders via FEIF/WorldF�engur, conservation organisations, policy makers if EU membership discussion continues)
- IP: no IP issues; all data open access

### 6. MSCA Section 3.1 — Work packages and Gantt chart (EXISTS AS PHASES IN RANNÍS but needs restructuring)

The Rannís draft has three phases (Phase 1 lab, Phase 2 ancestry/demography, Phase 3 adaptation). For MSCA, these need to be restructured into named Work Packages with:
- WP number and name
- Tasks within each WP
- Numbered milestones (M1, M2...) and deliverables (D1, D2...)
- A Gantt chart showing all WPs, milestones, deliverables, conferences, and training activities across 24 months

Suggested structure (24 months):

| WP | Name | Months | Key deliverables |
|---|---|---|---|
| WP1 | Laboratory | 1–10 | Libraries prepared, screening complete |
| WP2 | Ancestry and founding (Aim 1) | 9–18 | Imputed dataset, Aim 1 analyses complete |
| WP3 | Demographic history (Aim 2) | 12–24 | Ne reconstruction, ROH/IBD analyses complete |
| WP4 | Dissemination | ongoing | Papers submitted, data deposited, public engagement |

Each WP should have 3–5 numbered milestones and deliverables. Include a risk assessment (what if endogenous DNA is too low? → large sample pool; what if deCODE agreement changes? → CEH lab as fallback).

### 7. MSCA Section 3.2 — Host institution capacity (EXISTS IN RANNÍS Section D but not formatted correctly)

For MSCA this section appears TWICE:
- In Part B-1 Section 3.2: very short (~250 words), just states what the host offers
- In Part B-2 Section 5.2: a template TABLE with four rows (general description, supervisor profile, facilities, prior EU involvement)

For Tom Gilbert and CEH, this section should cover:
- CEH / Globe Institute description (ranking, aDNA infrastructure, HPC cluster, sequencing centre)
- Tom Gilbert's profile (h-index, key papers, especially the horse genome, prior MSCA fellows hosted)
- Facilities (ancient DNA lab, post-PCR lab, sequencing, computing)
- Prior EU funding (any Horizon/FP7 grants, prior MSCA fellows)

Check how many MSCA fellows Tom Gilbert has hosted — this is a selling point.

---

### 8. MSCA mobility justification (DOES NOT EXIST IN RANNÍS)

MSCA requires that you have NOT resided or worked in Denmark for more than 12 months in the 36 months before the deadline. Confirm this is the case. Also add a short paragraph (within Section 1.3 or 1.1) explaining why the host country and institution are the right choice — this is usually woven into the training justification rather than standing alone.

---

### 9. Ethics section (DOES NOT EXIST IN RANNÍS)

A short paragraph (one page maximum) stating:
- The work involves ancient horse skeletal material (not human remains) — therefore it does not fall under human tissue legislation
- Sampling permission has been granted by the National Museum of Iceland
- No personal data on living individuals is involved
- Export of specimens: note that the MSCA application states specimens travel to CEH Copenhagen (Minjasafnanefnd permission needed; Pálsdóttir et al. precedent for exporting to Norway)
- The standard EU ethics self-assessment form will need to be completed in the administrative section

## What needs to be changed (not just added)

1. **Duration throughout**: change all "36 months" and "3-year" references to "24 months"
2. **Host institution**: replace all Rannís references to deCODE as primary host with CEH Copenhagen (Tom Gilbert) as primary host; deCODE becomes a secondary resource / collaboration partner
3. **Aim 3**: either remove or significantly reduce in scope
4. **Framing**: MSCA is primarily about YOUR DEVELOPMENT as a researcher; Rannís is primarily about the project. The opening of the MSCA application should foreground why you specifically need this fellowship, not just why the science is important
5. **"First time" claim**: make this explicit and prominent early in Section 1.1 (all three example applications do this in the first page)
6. **European Research Area framing**: add a sentence or two about how the project contributes to European science, not just Icelandic science — MSCA is an EU programme

## MSCA deadline

The MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowships call is annual. The **2025 call deadline was September 2025** (likely already passed). The **2026 call deadline will be around September 2026** — the exact date is published when the call opens, typically in spring. Based on the memory notes for this project, the target is **September 2026**.

Check the current call at: https://ec.europa.eu/info/funding-tenders/opportunities/portal/screen/programmes/horizon — search for "MSCA Postdoctoral Fellowships 2026."

## Summary: what to do and in what order

1. **Finish and submit the Rannís application first** — it is close to complete
2. **After submission, create an MSCA working draft** starting from the Rannís text:
   - Trim to 24 months, remove or reduce Aim 3
   - Replace deCODE host with CEH Copenhagen
   - Restructure phases into named WPs
3. **Write the three new sections**: Section 1.3 (training plan), Section 2.2 (dissemination), and the Gantt chart — these take the most time
4. **Expand Section 1.4 and 2.1** from Rannís Section G
5. **Prepare Part B-2**: CV (max 2 pages, Europass format), host capacity table (Tom Gilbert), ethics section
6. **Get letter of commitment from Tom Gilbert** (mandatory; must be uploaded with the application)
