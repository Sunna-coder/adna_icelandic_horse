# Letter to FEIF

Dear FEIF Secretariat,

I am writing to introduce a research project on the ancient genomic history of the Icelandic horse and to explore the possibility of collaboration with FEIF.

I am a researcher in biological anthropology at the University of Iceland and a Research Scientist at the National Museum of Iceland. My research focuses on ancient DNA from archaeological Icelandic horse specimens spanning 870–1900 CE. The project aims to produce the first comprehensive genomic dataset for the Icelandic horse from the founding event to the present day, addressing questions about the breed's origins, the genomic consequences of over 1,100 years of isolation, and the evolutionary history of traits including gaits, disease susceptibilities, and adaptation to the Icelandic environment.

The findings of this project are directly relevant to FEIF's mandate. In particular, the project will establish the first historical baseline for disease-associated alleles — including those linked to sweet itch and ocular anomalies — and provide a scientific foundation for understanding current levels of genetic diversity in the breed. WorldFengur's health records and pedigree data represent an outstanding resource for connecting these genomic findings to the living horse population.

I am writing to ask whether FEIF would consider providing a letter of support for this project, and to enquire whether FEIF has any funding schemes or data-sharing agreements that might be relevant to this work. I would very much welcome the opportunity to discuss the project further.

Yours sincerely,

Dr Sigríður Sunna Ebenesersdóttir
Adjunct Professor, Faculty of Social and Human Sciences, University of Iceland
Research Scientist, National Museum of Iceland
